--- WHERE WE ARE ---

- We ran the ENTIRE Augsburg Confession through the pipeline (all 70 chunks,
  28 articles) in Hungarian
- Three batches: chunks 1-3, then 4-25, then 26-70
- Everything ran successfully — translation, evaluation, improvement loops, all
  of it end-to-end


--- THE NUMBERS ---

  Batch 1 (chunks 1-3):     avg 4.46 / 5.0
  Batch 2 (chunks 4-25):    avg 4.78 / 5.0
  Batch 3 (chunks 26-70):   avg 4.72 / 5.0

  Overall across all 70:    ~4.72 / 5.0
  Perfect 5.0 scores:       7 chunks total
  Above 4.5:                ~62 out of 70
  Below 4.0:                1 chunk (054, scored 3.75)
  JSON parse failures:      1 (chunk 019, but the translation itself was fine)

  Doctrinal accuracy:       Almost all chunks hit 5.0
  Terminology consistency:  Almost all chunks hit 5.0
  Clarity:                  Mostly 4.0-5.0
  Naturalness:              Consistently the weakest — almost always 4.0


--- WHAT'S WORKING ---

1. TRANSLATOR is strong on first pass
   - 90%+ of chunks needed ZERO improvement loops
   - Glossary enforcement is working — terms are being used correctly

2. VERIFIED IMPROVEMENT SYSTEM works
   - Glossary regression detector blocked bad "improvements" multiple times
   - Score regression guard reverted to best version when scores dropped
   - When improvements were accepted, scores went UP (e.g. chunk 013: 3.12 → 4.65)

3. DUAL-PASS EVALUATION is consistent
   - Very few tiebreakers needed
   - Two evaluation passes almost always agreed within 1 point

4. BACK-TRANSLATION confirms accuracy
   - Meaning preserved on every single chunk
   - All differences rated as "minor" — no doctrinal content lost


--- WHERE WE NEED TO IMPROVE ---

1. NATURALNESS (biggest gap)
   - Almost every chunk scores 4.0 on naturalness
   - The AI prioritizes accuracy over flow — correct tradeoff, but we can do better
   - PROPOSAL: Add a "naturalness polish" pass — a second AI call that smooths
     the Hungarian phrasing WITHOUT touching glossary terms or doctrine

2. GLOSSARY AMBIGUITIES (causes critical errors)
   - "devil" singular vs "devils" plural — glossary says "ördögök" (plural) but
     some passages clearly mean Satan (singular "ördög")
   - A few compound glossary entries conflict with individual entries
   - PROPOSAL: Have the team (Jonathan? a Hungarian speaker?) review the glossary
     for context-dependent terms

3. CHUNK 054 (lowest score — 3.75)
   - Needs investigation — 2 critical errors
   - May be a complex passage that needs special handling

4. ONE JSON PARSE FAILURE (chunk 019)
   - Translation was actually fine, just the eval report has a gap
   - Low priority fix
